Hybrid Rocket Static Fire Test Stand ‚Äì Wiring & Instrumentation Upgrade

Electrical system redesign for a hybrid rocket static fire test stand, focused on improved organization, signal integrity, and reduced measurement noise during long-duration and high-thrust testing.

Project Overview

Designed and implemented a new wiring architecture and electrical layout for a hybrid rocket static fire test stand. The upgrade improved cable organization, reduced electrical noise in sensor measurements, and simplified troubleshooting during test operations.

Wiring Diagram

Wiring diagram illustrating sensor routing, grounding strategy, and separation of high-current and low-level signal lines.

Objectives

  • Improve organization and readability of test stand wiring.
  • Reduce electrical noise affecting load cell and pressure transducer signals.
  • Separate high-current ignition and actuation circuits from low-level sensor wiring.
  • Enable faster troubleshooting and safer test operations.

My Role & Contributions

  • Designed the complete wiring diagram for the upgraded static fire test stand.
  • Organized sensor, power, and control wiring into clearly defined paths and bundles.
  • Implemented grounding and routing strategies to minimize electromagnetic interference.
  • Integrated instrumentation wiring for load cells, pressure transducers, and ignition systems.
  • Supported test operations by validating wiring integrity and improving signal quality.

Outcomes & Results

  • Noticeable reduction in electrical noise in sensor data.
  • Cleaner and more repeatable load cell and pressure measurements.
  • Improved maintainability and faster issue identification during test campaigns.
  • Increased confidence in data quality during long-duration hybrid rocket burns.

Tools & Technologies

  • Instrumentation: Load cells, pressure transducers, ignition systems
  • Electrical: Wiring harnessing, grounding strategies, noise mitigation
  • Testing: Hybrid rocket static fire testing
  • Documentation: Wiring diagrams and system layouts
