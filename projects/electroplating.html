Copper Electroplating of 3D-Printed Hybrid Rocket Components

Project Overview

This project focused on developing and refining a copper electroplating process for 3D-printed polymer components used in hybrid rocket motors. The goal was to improve thermal resistance, erosion durability, and structural integrity during high-temperature static fire testing.

My Role & Contributions

  • Developed a repeatable copper electroplating process for 3D-printed thermoplastics.
  • Performed surface preparation including sanding, chemical cleaning, and conductive coating.
  • Controlled bath chemistry, current density, and plating time to optimize adhesion and thickness.
  • Characterized plating rate, coating uniformity, and adhesion quality.
  • Evaluated plated components after static fire tests to assess erosion resistance.

Outcomes & Results

  • Achieved uniform copper coatings with strong adhesion to printed substrates.
  • Demonstrated improved thermal durability during high-temperature rocket testing.
  • Established a scalable plating workflow for future propulsion hardware.

Tools & Technologies

  • Electroplating power supplies and chemical baths
  • 3D printing (ABS, ASA, PETG-based materials)
  • Hybrid rocket motor test facilities
  • Post-test inspection and failure analysis
