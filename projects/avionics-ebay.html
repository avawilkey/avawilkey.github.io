Avionics E-Bay Design for Rocket Recovery

A simplified avionics e-bay concept for reliable dual-deployment recovery: clean wiring paths, compact packaging, and robust mounting for flight computers, batteries, and cameras.

Project Overview

Designed a simplified avionics e-bay architecture to improve reliability and serviceability in a high-power rocket recovery system. The e-bay houses two flight computers (RRC2+ and RRC3), routes wiring to dedicated main and drogue sides, and supports faster turnaround between test/flight days.

Objectives

  • Reduce wiring complexity and minimize cross-routing between main and drogue deployment circuits.
  • Improve packaging for batteries, cameras, and flight computers in a compact tube volume.
  • Increase repeatability and ease of assembly for rapid field integration and troubleshooting.

My Role & Contributions

  • Created a simplified e-bay layout with dedicated wire paths to drogue and main sides.
  • Packaged RRC2+ and RRC3 flight computers within a 3D-printed structure for consistent mounting.
  • Placed batteries at the top for accessibility and mass distribution.
  • Integrated side-mounted camera provisions to support flight video capture.
  • Iterated the design for serviceability: faster access to connectors and cleaner routing for continuity checks.

Outcomes & Results

  • Improved wiring clarity by separating drogue/main harness routing.
  • Reduced assembly time and made pre-flight inspections easier.
  • Improved maintainability: components are accessible without tearing down the entire bay.

Tools & Technologies

  • Fabrication: 3D-printing (prototype structures and routing features)
  • Avionics: RRC2+, RRC3 flight computers
  • Integration: wiring harnessing, continuity verification, system layout
