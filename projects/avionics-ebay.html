Avionics E-Bay Design for Rocket Recovery

A simplified avionics e-bay architecture developed for high-power rocket dual-deployment recovery. The design emphasizes clean wiring paths, compact packaging, and serviceability during integration and field operations.

Project Overview

This avionics e-bay was designed to improve reliability and maintainability in a high-power rocket recovery system. The structure houses two independent flight computers (RRC2+ and RRC3), separates drogue and main deployment wiring, and enables fast access for continuity checks and troubleshooting.

Design Snapshot

Flight-day integration and pre-launch checks using the dual-deploy avionics e-bay.

Design Objectives

  • Separate drogue and main deployment wiring to reduce cross-routing risk.
  • Package avionics components efficiently within a constrained airframe diameter.
  • Improve serviceability and turnaround time during test and flight operations.

My Role & Contributions

  • Designed the complete e-bay architecture and internal layout.
  • Integrated RRC2+ and RRC3 flight computers into a 3D-printed mounting structure.
  • Implemented dedicated wiring paths for drogue and main deployment circuits.
  • Positioned batteries for accessibility and favorable mass distribution.
  • Iterated the design to improve inspection access and connector visibility.
